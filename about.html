About Us

Meet the team.

Nolan Hall

With his grandfather being a renowned woodworker in Northern Indiana, Nolan picked up a passion for lumber at a very young age. He started off helping his grandfather cut out clock pieces on a scroll saw and once he got his own set of tools, he started building everything he could think of. Nolan enjoys modern design with some mid-century influence. He loves working with enormous tree slabs that weigh upwards of 200lbs. His favorite projects usually start with him picking up freshly cut slabs from locally felled trees from a sawmill in North Georgia. He then drives them over to Monroe, GA to have them kiln dried and ready for flattening. He is experienced in electrical and plumbing systems as well, so he is happy to get wild with any design ideas you may have.

Nolan is also a volunteer construction supervisor with Habitat for Humanity, where he has contributed over 1000 hours leading roofing and framing teams. In 2014, he started a home remodeling company with his construction mentor and worked on everything from building structures to flood remediation. Now, Nolan prefers to work on fine furniture pieces, but he's happy to build on a budget, too.
